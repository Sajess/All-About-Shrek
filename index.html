Who is Shrek?

Shrek is a fictional ogre character created by American author William Steig. Shrek is the protagonist of the book of the same name, a series of films by DreamWorks Animation, as well as a musical. The name "Shrek" is a romanization of the Yiddish word שרעק (shrek), or שרעקלעך (shreklekh), related to the German Schreck and meaning "fear" or "fright".[2] In the films, Shrek was voiced by Mike Myers, and in the musical, he was played principally by Brian d'Arcy James.

Who is Donkey?

Donkey is a fictional character created by William Steig and adapted by DreamWorks Animation for the Shrek franchise. He is voiced by Eddie Murphy. Donkey is an anthropomorphic donkey and his appearance is modeled after a miniature donkey named Perry. He is depicted with grey fur, brown eyes, and a black mane.

Shrek, 2001

Shrek (2001) follows the story of a grumpy ogre named Shrek who sets off on a quest to rescue Princess Fiona from a dragon-guarded tower, in order to reclaim his swamp from the fairy tale creatures banished there by the evil Lord Farquaad. Alongside his chatty companion, Donkey, Shrek discovers that Fiona has a secret—she turns into an ogre at night due to a curse. As they journey together, Shrek and Fiona fall in love, and Shrek learns that true love isn't about appearance, but accepting each other for who they really are.

Shrek 2, 2004

In Shrek 2 (2004), Shrek and Fiona visit her parents, King Harold and Queen Lillian, where Shrek feels out of place as an ogre among royalty. The Fairy Godmother, who wants Fiona to marry her son Charming, schemes to break them up. Shrek takes a potion to become human, but Fiona, who also drinks it, turns back into an ogre. With help from Donkey (who turns into a stallion) and Puss in Boots, Shrek and Fiona defeat the Fairy Godmother's plan, realizing that their love for each other doesn't depend on appearances. They return to their swamp, happy together.

Shrek the Third, 2007

In Shrek the Third (2007), Shrek is reluctant to become king after King Harold's death, so he sets out to find Arthur, the true heir to the throne. Meanwhile, Prince Charming leads a rebellion to take over the kingdom. Shrek, Donkey, and Puss in Boots eventually find Arthur, a rebellious teenager, who rises to the challenge and takes the throne. With Charming's plans thwarted, Shrek returns to his swamp with Fiona, realizing that being himself is enough, and they look forward to starting their family.

Shrek forever after, 2010

In Shrek Forever After (2010), Shrek is struggling with a midlife crisis, feeling that his life has become predictable and boring after settling down with Fiona. In a moment of frustration, he meets Rumpelstiltskin, who offers him a deal to relive his wild, carefree days in exchange for one day from his past. Shrek takes the deal, but it backfires, creating an alternate reality where Shrek never existed, and Rumpelstiltskin rules the kingdom. Fiona is a fierce rebel leader, and Shrek must earn back her love while trying to restore the original timeline. With the help of Donkey and Puss in Boots, Shrek learns that true happiness comes not from escaping responsibilities, but from cherishing the life he has. He ultimately defeats Rumpelstiltskin and restores his life, reaffirming his love for Fiona and embracing his family.
